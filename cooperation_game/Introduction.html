{{ endblock }} {{ block content }} Sequential Cooperation Game Instructions

Overview

You will participate in a “Sequential Cooperation Game” with a total of n players (including yourself).

All n players will remain in the same group throughout the entire experiment.

Position Assignment

At the beginning of each round, you will be randomly assigned a position number from 1 to n.

o If you are the player with position number 1, your decision will be considered first, followed by the player with position number 2, and so on; If you are the player with position number 2, your decision will be considered second, followed by the player with position number 3, and so on.

Number of Rounds

The game will be played for a total of 10 rounds.

o After the first 5 rounds, there may or may not be additional information provided to help you make decisions for your larger earnings. You may choose whether to take advantage of the provided information or continue with your initial strategy for the remaining 5 rounds.

Decision in Each Round and Possible Outcomes

In each round, if you choose to quit: only when you are the first quitter (the player with smallest position number), you can receive k dollars. Other players earn 0, regadless of their own choices.

If you choose to cooperate, only when all the players choose to cooperate: you and each player will earn t dollars (t>k).

You will not be informed of your exact earnings at the end of each round. However, after the first 5 rounds, you may receive the all-cooperation round success rate of previous 5 rounds as the additional information, which could help guide your decisions in the remaining rounds.

Your Total Earnings

At the end of the experiment, your total earnings will be the sum of all payoffs across 10 rounds.

Please read these instructions carefully and use them to guide your choices.

If you have questions, ask the experimenter before the game starts.
